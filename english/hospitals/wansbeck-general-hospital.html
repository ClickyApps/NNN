01670 564154

Cashpoint machine
a cashpoint machine is situated at the main entrance of the hospital there may be a small charge to use it.

Catering/refreshment facilities (in the hospital)
The hospital has a coffee shop at the main entrance run by our Hospital Volunteer Service where you can get snacks, and hot drinks, as well as newspapers and magazines.

Opening Times:

  • Monday to Friday 9am-7pm
  • Saturday and Sunday 12midday-4pm

There is a restaurant on the first floor where you can get hot and cold food and refreshments.

Opening Times:

  • Monday to Friday 8.30am-4.30pm
  • Saturday and Sunday 9am-3pm
Vending machines are available.

Other shops on site/nearby
There is a second coffee shop, Cafe Central, by the south entrance near to maternity, open Monday to Friday 8am-5pm. There is a large ASDA supermarket in the centre of Ashington which is open 24 hours most days of the week.

Laundry facilities
None.

Religious and spiritual support
Our Chaplaincy team provides confidential comfort and support, the Chaplains are available to people of all faiths or none.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)
Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS) available during office hours: 0800 032 0202

By car

From the A189 Spine Road. Exit the Woodhorn roundabout signposted “Ashington”. At the next roundabout take the first exit, continue along, past Barley Rise, and you will arrive at the hospital.

Nearest car park
The nearest car park is in front of the south entrance. Disabled car parking facilities are available. Free car parking is available for parents of babies on the unit, visitors still need to pay parking charges.

By bus

Nearest bus stop
The nearest bus stop is at the front of the hospital.

By train

There is no train station at Ashington. The closest mainline station is Morpeth, which is six miles away. Check The Trainline for timetables of all services.

Visiting information

Parents
open visiting with baby’s own siblings.

Number of visitors
Four.

Restrictions
Visitors must be accompanied by a parent. 2pm-7.30pm for all other family and friends. No other children under 16 years of age may visit.

Hygiene and infection prevention
Handwashing/gel on entry and exit to unit. We ask that all outdoor garments are removed on entering the unit.

Lockers
Lockers are available to store bags and valuables

Neonatal unit facilities

Access to a midwife
Available on site 24 hours a day

Parent accommodation
There are two parents rooms each with en-suite facilities.

Parent facilities
There is a sitting room with kettle, fridge and TV.

Facilities for siblings
Siblings are welcome, but must be supervised, toys, books and TV are available.

Mobile phone use
We advise mobile phones are turned off during visits and are used only for taking photos of own infant.

Telephone access
A public telephone is available for parents to use.

Breastfeeding facilities and support

  • breastfeeding support is available 24hours
  • breastfeeding community team
  • a breast pump is available to loan and there is storage for breast milk.

Baby rest time
we provide individualised care.

Interpreting Service
An interpreting service is available.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
The unit is at the south entrance to hospital on the 1st floor. There are stairs and a lift. Turn right as you exit the stairs/lift. There is a security door at the entrance to the unit, you will need to press the call button and identify yourself before you are allowed access to the unit.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Duty Advanced Neonatal Nurse Practitioner or allocated nurse for your baby. This information is available on the white boards above each cot space.

Ward round
There is a daily ward round, by the duty ANNP, at 830am.

Talking to the medical team
An appointment can be arranged to see the visiting consultant.
